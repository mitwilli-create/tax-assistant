2. Federal Compliance Requirements
2.1 Income Reporting
All income from all sources must be reported on Form 1040, regardless of whether a Form 1099 was received. The Tax Assistant must verify completeness against all known income sources before filing is initiated.

W-2 Wage Income
IRS Publication 525 | Taxable and Nontaxable Income: Wages, salaries, and tips are fully includible in gross income under IRC Section 61.
Box 1 wages ($335,340.44) are entered on Form 1040, Line 1a
Box 12 Code AA (Roth 401k contributions: $6,837.84) are post-tax and do not reduce Box 1
Box 12 Code D (Traditional 401k: $14,886.60) is pre-tax and already excluded from Box 1
Box 12 Code DD (employer health coverage: $11,574.61) is informational only — not taxable
Box 14 WAVPFLEE/WAVPMLEE are Washington state leave fund entries — not deductible federally

RSU Income
IRS Publication 525 | Stock Options and Other Equity Compensation: RSU income is ordinary income recognized at vesting, already included in W-2 Box 1.
IRC Section 83(b) | Property Transferred in Connection with Performance of Services: FMV at vesting date becomes the cost basis for subsequent capital gain calculation.
COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENT
Verify that Schwab reports the adjusted (stepped-up) cost basis on Form 1099-B for all RSU lot dispositions. The cost basis should equal the FMV at vesting, which was already taxed as ordinary income via the W-2. Failure to use the correct basis results in double taxation.

IRA Distribution
IRS Publication 590-B | Distributions from Individual Retirement Arrangements: Early distributions (before age 59.5) are subject to ordinary income tax plus 10% additional tax under IRC Section 72(t), unless an exception applies.
Distribution code 1 on 1099-R confirms early distribution with no known exception
$333.33 is includible in gross income on Form 1040, Line 4b
10% additional tax applies: $33.33 penalty reported on Schedule 2, Line 8
Review IRC Section 72(t)(2) exceptions before finalizing — qualified higher education, first-time homebuyer, disability, and others may apply

Capital Gains (1099-B — Pending)
IRS Publication 550 | Investment Income and Expenses: Capital gains and losses from stock sales are reported on Form 8949 and Schedule D.
Short-term gains (held 12 months or less) taxed as ordinary income
Long-term gains (held more than 12 months) taxed at preferential rates: 0%, 15%, or 20%
At Mitchell's income level ($335K+), the applicable long-term rate is 15% (below $518,901 single threshold for 2025)
Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT): 3.8% applies to net investment income above $200,000 (single). Dividends and capital gains are subject to NIIT.
IRC Section 1411 | Imposition of Tax: NIIT of 3.8% applies to lesser of net investment income or the excess of MAGI over threshold.

Dividend Income
IRS Publication 550 | Dividend Income: Qualified dividends meeting holding period requirements are taxed at preferential capital gains rates.
Total ordinary dividends: $195.00 — reported on Form 1040, Line 3b
Qualified dividends: $195.00 — reported on Form 1040, Line 3a; taxed at 15% rate
Verify with Schwab 1099-DIV that holding periods were satisfied for qualified treatment

3. Illinois Nonresident Compliance
3.1 Filing Obligation
Illinois Department of Revenue | Publication 102: Sales, Use, and Other Taxes: Nonresidents must file Form IL-1040 if they earned income from Illinois sources.
Mitchell is required to file Illinois Form IL-1040 (nonresident) because Google withheld Illinois income tax ($2,080.54), reflecting wages earned while physically working in Illinois during 2025.

3.2 Illinois Wage Allocation
IL W-2 Box 16 State Wages: $42,031.41 — represents wages Google allocated to Illinois based on days worked in state
IL W-2 Box 17 State Tax Withheld: $2,080.54
Illinois taxes nonresident income at 4.95% flat rate on Illinois-source income
Expected IL tax on $42,031.41: approximately $2,081.56 — withholding closely matches liability
35 ILCS 5/304 | Illinois Income Tax Act — Allocation and Apportionment: Nonresident individuals are taxed only on income from Illinois sources, including wages earned for services performed in Illinois.

COMPLIANCE NOTE
Illinois wages ($42,031.41) represent income actually earned while Mitchell was physically present in Illinois. This figure should not be modified. If the W-2 Box 16 figure appears inconsistent with actual days worked in Illinois, contact Google payroll for a corrected W-2 before filing.

4. New York Nonresident Compliance
4.1 Filing Obligation
NY Publication 88 | General Tax Information for New York State Nonresidents: Nonresidents who earn New York source income must file Form IT-203.
Mitchell must file New York Form IT-203 (nonresident) because Google withheld New York income tax ($680.28), confirming NY-source income was earned in 2025.

4.2 New York Wage Allocation — Critical Issue
HIGH-RISK COMPLIANCE AREA
The NY W-2 Box 16 shows $335,340.44 (the full federal wage amount). This is NOT the correct NY-source wage figure. Entering the full W-2 Box 1 amount as NY wages would result in approximately $19,000 in excess New York tax liability. The correct NY-sourced wage must be calculated from the withholding amount.

Correct NY wage allocation methodology:
The NY withholding of $680.28 divided by the applicable NY effective rate implies a NY-sourced wage of approximately $10,000
This figure should be entered as the NY nonresident wage in FreeTaxUSA's IT-203 section
If FreeTaxUSA generates an alert to revert to $335,340.44, evaluate and override — the back-calculated figure is correct

NY Tax Law Section 631 | New York Source Income of a Nonresident Individual: For employees of multi-state employers, NY-source wages are determined by the ratio of days worked in NY to total days worked.
NY IT-203-B Instructions | Allocation of Wages Earned Partly in and Partly Outside New York State: Use Form IT-203-B to allocate wages based on working days inside and outside New York.

4.3 New York Tax Rates (2025)
New York imposes a graduated income tax on nonresident income from NY sources. Key rates for 2025:
$0 - $17,150: 4.00%
$17,151 - $23,600: 4.50%
$23,601 - $27,900: 5.25%
$27,901 - $161,550: 5.85%
$161,551 - $323,200: 6.85%
Above $323,200: 9.65% (but only on NY-sourced income of ~$10,000)
